Research Summary

Pseudo biofeedback, giving manipulated feedback of biosignals, is attracting researchers' attention to regulate our emotions, especially anxiety and stress. Some previous research has shown that displaying visual/auditory/haptic stimuli at a slower heart rate than the actual rate could reduce the anxiety level of participants. As replication research, I developed a tool to display visual and auditory stimuli at a slower/faster heart rate. In addition, I further developed a device that heats and cools a Peltier element according to users' heart rates. A small user study indicated that receiving thermal feedback was more relaxing, while hearing heartbeat sounds was stressful.

My role

As a junior in college, I conducted this project as a short-term lab experience, a part of a lecture. Although I only had a little experience in Arduino, sensors, and Processing, I conducted a literature survey and developed the above tools within a month. I also ran a small user study to test whether this tool affects users' heart rates and emotions. I summarized these results and presented them at a regular laboratory meeting at the end of the one-month project.
